🧪 Chemical & Physical Change Notes

🔁 Change (Definition)

Any alteration in the shape, size, state or composition of a substance is called Change.

📚 Classification of Change

  • ⚙️ Physical Change
  • 🧪 Chemical Change

⚙️ Physical Change

A change in which only physical properties change and no new substance is formed.

Examples:
  • 🧊 Ice melting
  • 💧 Water freezing
  • 📄 Paper cutting

🧪 Chemical Change

A change in which new substance is formed with different properties.

Examples:
  • 🔥 Burning of wood
  • 🧲 Rusting of iron
  • 🍳 Cooking food

⚖️ Difference Between Physical & Chemical Change

Physical Change Chemical Change
No new substance New substance formed
Usually reversible Usually irreversible
Only physical properties change Chemical properties change

⚗️ Chemical Equation

A chemical equation represents a chemical reaction using symbols and formulas.

Example:

H₂ + O₂ → H₂O

📝 Word Chemical Reaction

Hydrogen + Oxygen → Water

⚖️ Balancing Chemical Equation

Balancing means making the number of atoms equal on both sides.

Example:

Unbalanced → H₂ + O₂ → H₂O

Balanced → 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Steps

  • Count atoms on both sides
  • Add coefficients
  • Make atoms equal

🔬 Types of Chemical Reactions

1️⃣ Combination Reaction

Two substances combine to form one product.

Example:

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

2️⃣ Decomposition Reaction

A compound breaks into simpler substances.

Example:

CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂

3️⃣ Displacement Reaction

One element replaces another element.

Example:

Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu

4️⃣ Double Displacement Reaction

Exchange of ions between compounds.

Example:

AgNO₃ + NaCl → AgCl + NaNO₃

🔥 Characteristics of Chemical Change

  • Heat produced 🔥
  • Gas produced 💨
  • Color change 🎨
  • Precipitate formed ⚗️
Example: Rusting of iron

⚙️ Characteristics of Physical Change

  • No new substance formed
  • Usually reversible
  • Only shape/size/state change
Example: Ice melting
